4.9.1 Measures and units

The definition of constructions objects need a definition of the underlying measures and units of their properties.

A comparison of two things always requires a criterion by which the comparison is made. This must be a characteristic (or property) that is inherent in both things. A characteristic is called a physical quantity if it has a value so that the ratio of two characteristic values is a real numerical factor. A comparison based on a quantity is thus quantifiable. The process of comparison to determine the numerical factor is called measurement. The measurability of a characteristic, i.e. the specification of a clear and reproducible measurement specification for a comparison, is equivalent to the definition of a physical quantity. The measure is the expression of this physical quantity.

Units are the expression of measured physical quantities and shall be associated to the Unit (IfcUnit) entity. They are strictly connected to property definition and propety values of construction objects. Measure and units can refer to test methods, that are defined according to harmonized Standards under the Construction Product Regulation (UE) n. 305/2011.

The definition of the units of values can be stored in Unit Assignment (IfcUnitAssignment) or directly in relation to each property template or property. The Unit Assignment( IfcUnitAssignment) is assigned to each IfcProject and/or IfcProjectLibrary instance in the data model. The implementation of the measures and unit in the product file has to follow the rules of EN ISO 16739-1:2018. Therefore, all unit shall be SIUnit (IfcSiUnit), Derived Unit(IfcDerivedUnit) and/or Conversion Based Unit (IfcConversionBasedUnit) according to the following chapters.

Every property template for a numeric value has to define its underlying measure and unit. The definition of measures and units shall follow the codes (TypicalUnitCod) in the table according to the appended table „Table of measures and units” of this standard. This table provides a guide for systems and data, maintaning semistructured or no-structured data, to create structured IFC-definitions of units and measures. The content of the table is not exhaustive. In the cases, where a unit that is needed, cannot be found in the table, this unit has to be documented according to the table before using it in construction objects data.

Table of measures and units

If a property in the template needs a unit, that is not includes in the Table of measures and units , the unit has to be defined as a SIUnit (IfcSiUnit), a Derived Uni(IfcDerivedUnits) (possibly in combination with a Conversion Based Unit(IfcConversionBasedUnit) to express the original unit as closely as possible.
